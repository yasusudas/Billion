Pilot map

Define the boundary before using live inquiries

  1. Classify inquiries: Separate hours, booking candidates, changes, callbacks, pricing, complaints, and urgent cases.
  2. Assign allowed actions: Decide what the AI may draft, summarize, route, or suggest without final authority.
  3. Set fallback rules: Route uncertainty, complaints, payment issues, medical/safety/legal topics, and high-impact decisions to a human.
  4. Write prohibited responses: List phrases, confirmations, promises, and decisions the AI must not make.
  5. Check acceptance: Test escalation, tone, logging, handoff, and stop conditions before production use.

Pilot risks

Issues to catch early

Final decisions

The AI should not confirm bookings, refunds, diagnoses, safety decisions, legal positions, or payment outcomes.

Missing handoff

A pilot is not ready if the team cannot define when a human receives the case and what context is handed over.

Unmeasured quality

Track accuracy, escalation fit, tone, fallback usage, and stop reasons before expanding the workflow.
