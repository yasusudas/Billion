Checklist

Five questions to answer before rollout

  1. Owner: Who is responsible for the workflow, model output review, exceptions, and stop decisions?
  2. Boundary: What data classes, source systems, and user prompts are allowed or prohibited?
  3. Approval: Which outputs require human review before they change a customer, employee, vendor, or financial state?
  4. Evidence: What input, output, review, and correction evidence must be retained for operational review?
  5. Fallback: What happens when the workflow is uncertain, unavailable, wrong, or outside scope?

Common gaps

Signals that a workflow needs review

Unclear authority

The AI system can draft, route, approve, or escalate work, but nobody owns the final decision boundary.

Hidden data exposure

Prompts include customer, contract, employee, or operational details without a clear input policy.

No stop condition

The team can describe expected success but not the conditions that pause the workflow or require rollback.
